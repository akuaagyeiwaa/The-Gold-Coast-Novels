The intellectual elite of the Gold Coast, driven by a desire for self-representation and public discourse, frequently engaged in literary and political writing across various periodicals. However, due to the subversive or anti-colonial tone of many of these writings, they were often excluded from mainstream colonial publications such as The Gold Coast Times and The Gold Coast Press. In response, James Hutton Brew established The Western Echo, a private newspaper based in Abura Dunkwa, which later became known as The Gold Coast Echo. This publication provided a vital platform for the Gold Coast intelligentsia to circulate their ideas through essays, fictional narratives, and serialized novels. Notably, Marita or the Folly of Love was serialized in this newspaper across approximately forty installments between 1885 and 1888.
