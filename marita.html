Author Biography

The author of Marita or the Folly of Love employed the pseudonym ‚ÄúA Native‚Äù to signal that the work was authored by an indigenous inhabitant of the Gold Coast colony. This pseudonymous authorship is widely interpreted as a strategic decision, given the critical stance the novel adopts toward colonial governance and societal norms. Scholarly opinion remains divided regarding the true identity of the author. While several researchers‚Äîincluding Jenkins (1985), Baku (1987), and Gocking (1999)‚Äîattribute the work to J. E. Casely Hayford, others have challenged this attribution. Yitah and Dako, for example, assert that "A. Native was an erudite scholar" (362) and, based on a close analysis of the text‚Äôs language, imagery, and stylistic features, contend that James Hutton Brew, rather than Casely Hayford, authored the novel (Yitah and Dako 362)

.

The intellectual elite of the Gold Coast, driven by a desire for self-representation and public discourse, frequently engaged in literary and political writing across various periodicals. However, due to the subversive or anti-colonial tone of many of these writings, they were often excluded from mainstream colonial publications such as The Gold Coast Times and The Gold Coast Press. In response, James Hutton Brew established The Western Echo, a private newspaper based in Abura Dunkwa, which later became known as The Gold Coast Echo. This publication provided a vital platform for the Gold Coast intelligentsia to circulate their ideas through essays, fictional narratives, and serialized novels. Notably, Marita or the Folly of Love was serialized in this newspaper across approximately forty installments between 1885 and 1888.
